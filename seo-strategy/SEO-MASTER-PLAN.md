## 1. Executive summary — the honest version

You **cannot** rank #1 for *every* keyword at once from Domain Authority 1. Anyone who promises that is selling hype. What you **can** do — and what this plan delivers — is a **sequenced assault**:

1. **Win the winnable commercial keywords first** (low-difficulty "near me" + category×city terms where even shadiyana ranks at SEO-difficulty 8–18).
2. **Out-cover shadiyana where it's thin** — non-venue categories (photographers, makeup, catering, mehndi, decor), tier-2 cities, Urdu, and the events they ignore (Mayun, Dholki).
3. **Build authority** (backlinks + PR + GEO) so you climb to the contested head terms ("wedding venues", "best wedding photographers in karachi") within 6–12 months.

**The single most important fact:** weddingwala.pk already has the *right site architecture*. The gap is **authority and content depth, not structure.** So this plan is ~70% content + links + indexation, ~30% page/template work.

## 4. The strategic wedge — how DA 1 beats DA 22

We do **not** fight them head-on for "wedding venues Karachi" in month one. We win on **four asymmetric advantages**, in order:

1. **Vendor-name navigational pages.** Build a rich, schema-marked profile page for *every* photographer, caterer, makeup artist, venue, etc. You rank #1 for "{vendor name}" almost uncontested (low difficulty, branded intent). This is how shadiyana bootstrapped — and it compounds.
2. **Under-served categories × cities.** Photographers, makeup, catering, mehndi, decor — especially in tier-2 cities — where the incumbents are weak/absent. Low difficulty, real commercial intent.
3. **Format superiority (GEO).** Answer-first **"Best {vendor} in {city} 2026"** listicles + comparison tables + FAQ schema. Shadiyana has *directory listings* but not *citable articles* — this is what AI Overviews/ChatGPT/Perplexity cite (listicles = 63% of AI citations; tables cited ~2.5× more).
4. **Tools + cost content.** Crawlable PKR budget calculator + annual "Wedding Cost in Pakistan {year}" guide + city cost pages — the most-linked, highest-intent informational format, and we already host the tool.

## 6. Page & content plan

### 6A. Money pages (already scaffolded — deepen, don't rebuild)
- **Vendor category × city** (`/wedding-photographers/[city]` etc. — exists): ensure each has (a) ≥6–10 real vendor cards, (b) unique intro copy per city (not templated boilerplate — Google demotes thin programmatic pages), (c) PKR price ranges, (d) ratings/reviews, (e) FAQ block, (f) internal links to related categories + events in that city.
- **Vendor profile pages** (per-vendor): the navigational-keyword flywheel. Rich profile + portfolio + reviews + price + `LocalBusiness`/`AggregateRating` schema. **This is the highest-ROI page type to mass-produce.**
- **Thin-page guard:** city×category pages with <3 vendors → `noindex` until populated, to avoid index bloat that drags the whole domain.

### 6B. "Best of" listicle layer (the GEO weapon — highest priority new content)
For each Tier-1 city × core-6 vendor: **"Best {vendor} in {city} 2026 — Prices, Reviews & Packages"** as an answer-first article (direct answer in first 40–60 words, numbered list, each entry = name + specialty + PKR range + rating, comparison table, FAQPage schema, visible "last updated"). This is what AI engines cite and what converts. ~24 articles (6 vendors × 4 cities) = Phase-1 content sprint.

### 6C. Cost / budget content (most-linkable format)
- **Pillar:** "Wedding Cost in Pakistan 2026 — Full PKR Breakdown" (tiered totals: middle 1.5–3.5M, upper-middle 4–7M, elite 8–15M+; line items: venue, catering per head, photography, decor, makeup, dress; 15–20% YoY inflation hook).
- **City variants:** "Wedding Cost in Lahore/Karachi/Islamabad 2026".
- **Interactive:** promote the existing `/planning-tools/budget` calculator hard — it's a link magnet competitors don't have on the web.

### 6D. Informational feeders (capture inspiration traffic → internal-link to money pages)
Mehndi photography poses/designs · barat & walima dress ideas · mayun decor ideas · nikkah decoration ideas · dholki song lists · "order of events at a Pakistani wedding" · per-event cost comparison ("Mehndi vs Barat vs Walima — which costs most"). Use the existing `/blog/[cluster]` engine.

### 6E. Link magnets (PR flywheel — builds the DA we lack)
1. **Annual "Decemberistan / Pakistan Wedding Season Report"** — original data (Google Trends + booking data). PK press (Business Recorder, Express Tribune, VOA, Dawn) runs this story *every year* — give them the dataset = editorial backlinks.
2. **Real-wedding features with full vendor credits** (`/real-weddings` exists) — every credited vendor links back = vendor backlink manufacturing. Pitch styled shoots to Mashion, Diva, Something Haute, Brides of Pakistan.
3. **"Best X in {city}" listicles** double as link bait (listed vendors link to them).

## 7. Backlinks, technical & GEO

**Backlinks (the #1 lever — 0 today):**
- Foundational citations: Google Business Profile, Bing Places (feeds ChatGPT local), local PK directories, UrduPoint, social profiles — consistent NAP.
- Vendor reciprocity: every listed vendor gets a profile → ask for a "Featured on Wedding Wala" link.
- Digital PR: the Decemberistan report + real weddings (above).
- Guest content / partnerships with PK wedding bloggers and photographers.

**Technical (audit & fix):**
- Connect **GSC + GA4** (step zero) → submit `sitemap.xml` (exists) → confirm indexation of city×category pages.
- Schema on every page type: `LocalBusiness` + `AggregateRating` (vendors), `ItemList` (listicles), `FAQPage` (city/cost pages), `Organization`, `BreadcrumbList`, `Article` (blog).
- Core Web Vitals (INP), mobile-first (most PK wedding search is mobile), fast LCP on listing pages.
- Keep AI crawlers (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended) **allowed** for GEO upside; add a low-cost `llms.txt`.

**GEO (AI search):** answer-first copy, comparison tables, FAQ schema, freshness ("2026" + last-updated, quarterly refresh), third-party mentions (stronger AI signal than backlinks). Riding the same listicle/comparison/FAQ pages from §6B.
